# Chapter 1: Brief Review of Band Structures

## Overview
This chapter provides a foundational review of band structures in condensed matter physics, which is essential for understanding topological phases.

## Key Topics

### 1. Bloch's Theorem
- Electrons in a periodic potential have wavefunctions that can be written as $u_k(r) e^{ik \cdot r}$
- The crystal momentum $k$ is a good quantum number

### 2. Band Structure
- Energy eigenvalues $E_n(k)$ form bands
- Bands are separated by band gaps
- Valence bands vs conduction bands

### 3. Band Insulators vs Metals
- Insulators: all bands below Fermi level are filled, gap to next band
- Metals: partially filled bands at Fermi level

### 4. Berry Phase
- Geometric phase acquired when parameters evolve cyclically
- Important for topological classification
